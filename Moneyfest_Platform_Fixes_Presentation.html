The Audit Discovery

The Fritz Case: How the books lied to us.

During the audit, I discovered our system was recording money that didn't physically exist.

The "Ghost Money" Cycle

  • 1Borrower pays ₱500 security deposit (Initial Capital).
  • 2Borrower goes overdue. Penalty of ₱260 applied.
  • 3THE BUG: System logged that ₱260 as new profit.
  • 4But the ₱260 was just a slice of the ₱500 we already had.
₱260 shown as earned · ₱0 physically received

The Illusion

Reported Earnings ₱435
Real Interest Profit ₱175 ✓
Phantom Penalty Income ₱260 ✗
Physical Cash Earned ₱175

By removing this bug, we ensured our "Ready to Withdraw" profit is always backed by real physical Pesos in GCash.

Protecting Our Reliability

Eliminating "Fake Late" Penalties.

Borrower trust depends on accuracy. Our audit found that borrowers who paid **on time** (the 5th or 20th) were being wrongfully charged ₱20 penalties.

This was due to a technical "Timezone Drift"—where our server in the US was recording a Philippine May 5th payment as May 4th, triggering an automatic late-fee.

RISK IDENTIFIED

On-time borrowers were losing credit score points and money, leading to disputes and loss of faith in our system.

RESOLUTION

I unified the entire engine to strict Philippine Standard Time (PHT). Now, early is early, and on-time is on-time.

The Financial Engine

Capital Rotation vs. True Profit

A ₱1,425 collection is not ₱1,425 of profit. To grow, we must strictly separate what we own from what we earn.

Pool A: Capital Rotation (Principal)

The original money lent. As it returns, it is automatically tagged to stay in the 'Vault' so we can immediately fund the next borrower's loan.

Pool B: True Profit (Interest)

The service fee earned. Our system now isolates this from the principal. **This is the ONLY money we account for as income.**

Why this matters:

By keeping these two pots of money separate, we never accidentally withdraw our principal. This guarantees that our lending capacity—the ability to keep giving out loans—remains untouched and grows over time.
